The Basics

Field, Ball, Players â–¶
  • Two teams of 11 players each (including a goalkeeper).
  • Standard match: 90 minutes (two 45-minute halves) plus stoppage time.
  • Ball in play unless the referee stops it or it leaves the field.
  • Subs permitted per competition rules (commonly 5 from the bench, used in 3 breaks).
Referees & VAR â–¶

The match is controlled by the referee, assistants, and fourth official. Some competitions use VAR for checkable incidents: goals, penalties, direct red cards, and mistaken identity. The on-field decision stands unless there is a clear and obvious error.

Equipment & Safety â–¶
  • Mandatory: shirt, shorts, socks, shin guards, boots. Colors distinguish teams and officials.
  • No jewelry. Headgear allowed if safe.

Offside (simplified)

When is a player offside? â–¶

A player is in an offside position if, at the moment a teammate plays the ball, they are in the opponentsâ€™ half and closer to the goal line than both the ball and the second-last opponent. Being in an offside position is not an offense on its own.

Offside offense occurs only if the player becomes involved in active play: interferes with play/an opponent or gains an advantage from the position.
No offside from a goal kick, throw-in, or corner kick.

Fouls, Misconduct & Cards

Direct free kick fouls â–¶

Penal (direct) fouls when careless/reckless/excessive force, e.g.:

  • kicks or attempts to kick; trips; jumps at; charges; strikes; pushes
  • tackles/impedes with contact; holds; spits; handles the ball deliberately (except goalkeeper in own area)

Indirect free kick offenses â–¶
  • Dangerous play without contact; impeding without contact.
  • Goalkeeper specific: holds the ball >6 seconds; handles after a deliberate kick from a teammate; double-touch on release; etc.
Disciplinary sanctions (cards) â–¶
  • Yellow card: caution for unsporting behavior, dissent, repeated fouls, delaying restarts, failing to respect distance, entering/re-entering without permission, etc.
  • Red card: serious foul play, violent conduct, spitting, denying an obvious goal-scoring opportunity (DOGSO), offensive/abusive language, second yellow.

Restarts

Kick-off & dropped ball â–¶
  • Kick-off: ball is in play when kicked and clearly moves; can go backward.
  • Dropped ball: to a single player if play stopped inside the penalty area (goalkeeper) or last touch location; others 4.5m (5yds) away.
Ball out of play: throw-in, goal kick, corner â–¶
  • Throw-in: when ball fully crosses touchline; both feet on/behind line, throw from behind and over the head.
  • Goal kick: when attackers last touch and ball crosses goal line (no goal); ball in play when kicked and clearly moves; opponents outside penalty area until then.
  • Corner kick: when defenders last touch and ball crosses goal line (no goal); ball in play when kicked and moves.
Free kicks & penalties â–¶
  • Direct vs indirect: indirect requires a second touch to score; referee raises arm until taken.
  • Wall distance: opponents at least 9.15m (10yds) from the ball unless on goal line between posts.
  • Penalty kick: ball on spot; goalkeeper on/over goal line until kick; attackers & defenders outside penalty area and arc, behind the ball.
